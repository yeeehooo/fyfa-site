About your instructor

Yee has watched the industry from the inside.

Yee, the founder of FYFA, is an investment writer and Chartered Financial Analyst living in Vancouver, Canada. He writes regularly for Seeking Alpha with a focus on quality businesses with wide moats, high efficiency, and strong cash flows.

A key purpose of his writing is to invite opposing views — which, he's found, are often essential to refining investment ideas. Yee previously worked for Fidelity International and Bloomberg in Singapore and Hong Kong.

Why this course

“As a financial analyst, why am I taking on my own profession?”

Instructor's Message

It took me years to figure out that the trade of investment advisory is a wealth detractor for most people. The industry on the whole is structurally flawed — misaligned interests between advisor and client, needless (and often toxic) complexity, excessive fees, and style over substance.

If a building is rotten at the foundation, the best thing to do is take it down.

That said, advisors aren't entirely without merit. Retail investors — especially those with little financial training — can be vulnerable to pitfalls and psychological biases. A high-caliber advisor may help them avoid disastrous outcomes.

But the blossoming of AI has rendered that risk-management role obsolete. AI has no fear, no greed, no ego. It follows pre-set rules. It can offer sound counsel in despair or in euphoria, and help steady your pace of investing regardless of market conditions. Assign clear roles to your AI agents, and they'll work like the best human advisors — for peanuts.
